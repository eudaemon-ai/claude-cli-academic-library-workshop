SAMPLE PATRON REFERENCE QUESTIONS
Library AI Workshop — Module 1: Reference & Research

These 10 questions range from straightforward to complex. Use Question 1 for Exercise 2 (Patron Query), Question 3 for Exercise 3 (Synthesis), and any question of your choice for Exercise 4 (Follow Up).

---

QUESTION 1 (Exercise 2 default)
"I need peer-reviewed sources about the mental health impacts of social media on college students, published in the last 5 years."

Patron context: Undergraduate student, writing a 10-page research paper for a psychology course.

---

QUESTION 2
"I'm looking for historical data on voter turnout rates in U.S. presidential elections from 1960 to 2020. I need something I can cite in a political science paper."

Patron context: Graduate student, needs data with citable source (not just a Wikipedia table).

---

QUESTION 3 (Exercise 3 default)
"Can you give me a brief overview of what the research says about positive vs. negative mental health outcomes of social media use, so I can frame my grant proposal?"

Patron context: Faculty member in Psychology, preparing an NIH grant application.

---

QUESTION 4
"I need information about Indigenous land rights in Canada — specifically about the legal frameworks after the 1982 Constitution Act. Do you have anything on this?"

Patron context: Law student, second year, familiar with Canadian constitutional law basics.

---

QUESTION 5
"Where can I find data on how many people are employed in the renewable energy sector in the United States? I need something from the last 2 years."

Patron context: MBA student writing a market analysis report.

---

QUESTION 6
"I'm trying to find the original source for the claim that 'it takes 10,000 hours to master a skill.' Can you help me verify if this is a real research finding?"

Patron context: Faculty member preparing a syllabus and wants to use (or debunk) the reference.

---

QUESTION 7
"Do you have textbooks or guides on project management methodologies — specifically Agile and Scrum? I need something practical, not too theoretical."

Patron context: Staff member in university IT department, preparing for a certification exam.

---

QUESTION 8
"I need to find out if there's any research on how first-generation college students experience library services differently from other students."

Patron context: Librarian conducting assessment, wants peer-reviewed literature for a journal article.

---

QUESTION 9
"I'm looking for reviews or critical analyses of a novel called 'Beloved' by Toni Morrison. I need secondary sources, not just plot summaries."

Patron context: Undergraduate student, English major, writing a literary criticism paper.

---

QUESTION 10
"Can you help me find demographic data on the aging population in rural U.S. counties? I need Census or government data, not commercial estimates."

Patron context: Public health graduate student, writing a thesis on rural healthcare access.
